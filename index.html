01 — About

About

I am a senior undergraduate at Kangnam University majoring in Computer Engineering with a double major in Data Science (AI track). I work at the intersection of machine learning and robotics, with a focus on adapting large pretrained vision-language models for embodied control.

My research interests are practical and systems-oriented: how can we reuse the rich priors inside frozen VLMs while learning small, robot-specific policies that actually run in real time on real hardware? I care about reproducible pipelines — from teleoperation and data collection, to flow-matched action heads, to deploying on edge devices like the Jetson AGX Orin.

02 — Research

Research Interests

  • Vision-Language-Action Models

    Reusing frozen vision-language priors and learning robot-specific action heads from demonstration data.

  • Robot Manipulation

    Pick-and-place and tool-use policies for 6-DoF arms; teleoperation, demonstration collection, and policy deployment.

  • Embodiment Transfer

    Designing pipelines that scale from one robot platform to another without retraining the entire stack.

  • Real-World Deployment

    Real-time inference and control on edge devices (Jetson AGX Orin) with ROS2 communication.

Currently working on

FlowBridge — Flow-Matched Manipulation VLA

A manipulation-focused VLA that connects frozen vision-language priors with a robot-specific action head, currently targeting the Dobot E6 with a suction gripper.
